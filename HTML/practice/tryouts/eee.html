Electric Motors and Generators

DC Motors

  • Series Motor: A DC motor with a field winding connected in series with the armature winding, known for its high starting torque.
  • Shunt Motor: A DC motor with a field winding connected in parallel with the armature winding, known for its constant speed and good speed regulation properties.
  • Compound Motor: A DC motor with both series and shunt field windings, which provides a combination of the characteristics of series and shunt motors.
  • Permanent Magnet Motor: A DC motor that uses permanent magnets to create the field flux, known for its simplicity and low cost.

AC Motors

  • Synchronous Motor: An AC motor that runs at a constant speed, synchronized with the frequency of the AC supply.
  • Induction Motor: An AC motor that uses electromagnetic induction to produce torque, known for its simplicity and low cost.
  • Asynchronous Motor: An AC motor that runs at a speed that is not synchronized with the frequency of the AC supply.
